# Blueprint MBSE

Blueprint is not yet another monolithic, licensed MBSE (Model Based Systems Engineering) tool.

Instead, Blueprint defines an AI ready text based toolchain that you can use to develop, debug, and maintain fully executable and platform independent models of your system's software requirements.

Blueprint focuses on the most critical part of your system: the required software behavior.

### Why this approach?

My name is Leon Starr and I have been helping Fortune 500 teams build models of
a wide variety of mission safety critical systems for over 25 years.

I've seen the big MBSE tools in action, on real projects. They provide massive amounts of model editing and management features.

But I've also seen system engineers wasting time pushing pixels and hopping around endless menu hierarchies. I've seen teams struggle with crappy configuration management. I've seen developers locked out of models at critical moments because they can't find a license key. And worse, I know the models get ignored downstream by key developers who don't want to waste their time with any of this mess.  As a developer myself, I get it, and I don't blame them.

I designed Blueprint to minimize modeling busy-work and put the focus on high impact modeling that solves the difficult and nuanced system specification problems at a truly useful level of detail (as opposed to vacuous boxes in boxes), and makes a real difference on a project.

And by focusing on human readable text rather than GUI's from the outset, Blueprint modules integrate nicely with AI agents.

### But I want diagrams!

Me too!  In fact I insist on beautiful, well laid out diagrams. With engineering quality annotation and title block layers and support for multiple notations. I got tired of wrestling with PlantUML and other drawing engines to produce the crisp, clear engineering layouts I needed. Blueprint offers sophisticated layout engines that avoid all that pain. So you get the best of both worlds - text and diagrams.
